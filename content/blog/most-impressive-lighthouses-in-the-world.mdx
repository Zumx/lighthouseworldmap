Some lighthouses are feats of engineering and endurance as much as navigation —
built where building should be impossible, or rising as architecture in their
own right. These are the world's lighthouse icons.

## Fastnet, Ireland

The "Teardrop of Ireland" — a granite tower on a wave-lashed Atlantic rock,
the last sight of home for emigrants, and a feat of Victorian sea engineering.

## Bell Rock, Scotland

The oldest surviving sea-washed lighthouse in the world, built on a reef
submerged at high tide — a legendary engineering triumph.

## Jeddah Light, Saudi Arabia

Generally cited as the tallest lighthouse in the world, a modern tower on a
Red Sea breakwater.

## Pharos of Alexandria, Egypt (lost)

One of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World — the lighthouse that gave its
name to all others, known only from history now.

## Tower of Hercules, Spain

The oldest Roman lighthouse still in use, working for nearly two millennia — a
UNESCO site.

## Phare du Créac'h, France

On Ushant — one of the most powerful lighthouses ever built, guarding a
ferocious shipping lane.

## La Jument, France

Famous worldwide from the photograph of a keeper at the door as a colossal wave
engulfs the tower.

## Cape Hatteras, USA

The tallest brick lighthouse in America, spiral-striped, and astonishingly
relocated bodily inland from the sea.

## Les Éclaireurs, Argentina

The "Lighthouse at the End of the World" in the Beagle Channel — remoteness
made iconic.

## Slettnes, Norway

The northernmost mainland lighthouse on Earth, under midnight sun and aurora.

## What makes a lighthouse impressive

Sometimes it is where it stands (Bell Rock, Fastnet, La Jument — built on rock
the sea owns), sometimes its scale or age (Jeddah, the Tower of Hercules), and
sometimes pure symbolism (the Pharos, the End of the World). The greatest
lighthouses are human defiance of the sea, made permanent.

## Visiting the icons

Many of the most dramatic (Fastnet, Bell Rock, La Jument) are offshore and
view-only or boat-access in calm weather — the spectacle is seeing them from
the sea. The accessible icons (Tower of Hercules, Cape Hatteras) are
climbable, ticketed, and busy in season. Plan around weather and crossings;
respect that the wild ones are wild for a reason.
