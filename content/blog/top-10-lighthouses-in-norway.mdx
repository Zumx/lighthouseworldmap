Norway's vast, jagged coast produced some of the most dramatic lighthouses
(*fyr*) anywhere — many now restored as remote guesthouses where you can spend
the night surrounded by the sea. These ten stand out.

## 1. Lindesnes Fyr

Norway's oldest and southernmost lighthouse, on a wild cape where the North Sea
meets the Skagerrak — a national lighthouse museum.

## 2. Kjeungskjær Fyr

A striking octagonal red tower standing alone on a skerry — one of Norway's
most photographed.

## 3. Utsira Fyr

A remote island light famous among birdwatchers and storm-watchers.

## 4. Runde Fyr

Above one of Norway's great seabird islands, with cliffs full of puffins.

## 5. Kråkenes Fyr

Clinging to a cliff facing the open ocean — you can stay overnight and feel the
storms.

## 6. Slettnes Fyr

The northernmost mainland lighthouse on Earth, on the Finnmark coast under the
midnight sun and aurora.

## 7. Tungenes Fyr

An accessible cultural-heritage light near Stavanger with a coastal museum.

## 8. Lista Fyr

On a flat, windswept peninsula renowned for migrating birds.

## 9. Ryvarden Fyr

A west-coast lighthouse turned cultural centre and guesthouse.

## 10. Andenes Fyr

A tall red tower on the edge of the Arctic, gateway to Vesterålen whale waters.

## How Norwegian lighthouse visits work

A remarkable number of Norwegian lighthouses have been preserved as
*fyrferie* — lighthouse-holiday guesthouses you can book a room or whole
station in. Others are museums or cultural centres. Many are genuinely remote:
boat or tidal-causeway access, limited services, and weather that dictates
everything.

## When to go

Summer (June–August) gives the midnight sun, accessible boats, and open
guesthouses; the far-north lights add aurora in the dark months for the
hardy. Autumn storm-watching from a snug lighthouse is a Norwegian tradition —
but check access, as crossings cancel in bad weather.

## Safety and respect

These coasts are ferocious — stay well back from exposed rock in swell, respect
boat-operator decisions absolutely, and follow seabird-colony rules in nesting
season (keep distance, no disturbance). Pack for sudden weather even in summer.
