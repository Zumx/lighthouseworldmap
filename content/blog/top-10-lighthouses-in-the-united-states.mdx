The US coastline is studded with historic lighthouses, many climbable, most
photogenic, and each with a story of storms and keepers. These ten are the
essentials.

## 1. Cape Hatteras, North Carolina

The tallest brick lighthouse in America, its black-and-white spiral barber-pole
the icon of the Outer Banks — and famously moved inland from the eroding sea.

## 2. Portland Head Light, Maine

Commissioned in the era of George Washington, set on rugged cliffs — perhaps
the most painted lighthouse in the country.

## 3. Split Rock, Minnesota

Perched dramatically on a Lake Superior cliff — a Great Lakes masterpiece.

## 4. Pigeon Point, California

One of the tallest on the US West Coast, a classic white tower on a Pacific
bluff.

## 5. Bodie Island, North Carolina

Another striking Outer Banks tower, horizontally banded and climbable.

## 6. St. Augustine, Florida

A spiral-striped tower over the nation's oldest city, with sweeping coastal
views.

## 7. Heceta Head, Oregon

Often called the most photographed lighthouse in the US, on a fog-wrapped
headland.

## 8. Bass Harbor Head, Maine

A small light clinging to pink granite in Acadia — a sunset legend.

## 9. Cape Disappointment, Washington

Guarding the treacherous mouth of the Columbia, on the "Graveyard of the
Pacific."

## 10. Yaquina Head, Oregon

The tallest on the Oregon coast, ringed by tide pools and seabird colonies.

## How US lighthouse visits work

Many are run by the National Park Service, state parks, or preservation
societies. Some you can climb (Hatteras, Bodie Island, St. Augustine) for a fee
on a timed ticket; others are exterior-only or have keeper's-quarters museums.
Climbing towers often close in high wind or extreme heat.

## When to go

Late spring through early autumn gives the best access and weather; climbable
towers may have shorter shoulder-season hours. Fog is part of the
Pacific-coast experience — and the point of the light. Sunrise and sunset
deliver the classic photographs.

## Safety and etiquette

Tower stairs are steep, narrow, and hot — heed posted health warnings, hold
the rail, and respect group limits. Stay back from cliff edges and never turn
your back on the surf on rocky Pacific and Maine shores ("sneaker waves" kill
visitors). Take only photos.
